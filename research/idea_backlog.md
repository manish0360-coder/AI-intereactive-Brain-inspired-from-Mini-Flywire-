# Research Candidate 001

## Name

Compositional Mental Construction

## Observation

Humans appear capable of constructing internal representations of previously unseen entities by combining partial features extracted from language and memory.

## Example

Input:

* Four legs
* Medium size
* Tail
* Deer-like face
* Multiple horns

Rather than retrieving a stored memory, the cognitive system composes a novel internal representation by integrating features from multiple existing concepts.

## Research Question

Can an artificial cognitive architecture construct useful internal representations of previously unseen entities through compositional reasoning rather than direct retrieval?

## Why This Matters

If successful, this mechanism could support imagination, hypothesis generation, zero-shot reasoning, concept formation, and design synthesis.
