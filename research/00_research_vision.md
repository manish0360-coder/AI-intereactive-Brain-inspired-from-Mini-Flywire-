# MiniFlyWire Research Vision

## Why MiniFlyWire Exists

Artificial intelligence has achieved extraordinary success by scaling computation, data, and model size. Modern systems can write software, solve mathematical problems, generate images, and assist with scientific discovery. Yet despite these capabilities, one fundamental question remains unanswered:

**How do increasingly structured, interpretable, and transferable internal representations emerge from experience?**

Current AI systems are primarily evaluated by their external behavior. They are judged by the quality of their outputs, benchmark scores, and task performance. While these metrics measure capability, they reveal very little about the internal computational processes that produced those results.

MiniFlyWire exists to shift the focus of AI research from **behavior** to **representation**.

Rather than asking *"Can the system solve the task?"*, MiniFlyWire asks:

* What internal knowledge structures emerged?
* Why did they emerge?
* Which computational mechanisms caused their formation?
* How do these representations evolve over time?
* Which mechanisms improve knowledge transfer, abstraction, and interpretability?
* How can these mechanisms be experimentally validated?

MiniFlyWire is therefore not designed primarily as an intelligent agent or a visualization system.

It is designed as a **scientific laboratory for artificial cognition**.

Its purpose is to make cognition observable, measurable, reproducible, and scientifically testable.

Every mechanism introduced into the architecture must satisfy four conditions:

1. It represents a clearly defined cognitive hypothesis.
2. It can be experimentally isolated from other mechanisms.
3. Its causal contribution can be quantitatively measured.
4. Its effectiveness can be falsified through controlled experiments.

The long-term objective is not merely to build increasingly capable artificial systems, but to establish a rigorous scientific methodology for understanding how artificial knowledge grows, reorganizes, stabilizes, and transfers across domains.

MiniFlyWire is the experimental foundation of that methodology.
