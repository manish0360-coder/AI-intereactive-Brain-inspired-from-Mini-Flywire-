# Section 1 — Mission

The mission of MiniFlyWire is to establish a scientifically rigorous experimental platform for investigating artificial cognition.

Rather than proposing a fixed cognitive architecture or claiming a predefined path toward artificial general intelligence, MiniFlyWire exists to systematically evaluate which computational mechanisms contribute to the emergence of increasingly structured, reusable, interpretable, and transferable knowledge.

Every cognitive mechanism introduced into the platform is treated as a scientific hypothesis rather than an engineering feature. Its value must be demonstrated through controlled experiments, reproducible benchmarks, causal analysis, and measurable evidence.

MiniFlyWire therefore serves as a research laboratory whose primary objective is not maximizing task performance, but improving scientific understanding of how artificial cognitive systems acquire, organize, construct, transfer, and apply knowledge through interaction with their environment.

The long-term contribution of MiniFlyWire is to provide a reproducible methodology for cognitive mechanism research, enabling future architectures—including Noetica and Mini Prometheus—to be built upon experimentally validated scientific foundations rather than intuition alone.

# Section 2 — North Star

**Which computational mechanisms are necessary and sufficient for an artificial system to acquire, organize, construct, transfer, and apply knowledge through continuous interaction with its environment, and how can their causal contributions be experimentally demonstrated?**
